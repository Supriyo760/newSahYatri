# SahYatri Medical Protocol Handbook

This handbook documents the standard operating procedures (SOPs) utilized by the SahYatri Dynamic First-Aid Decision Trees (`src/lib/medical/decision-trees.ts`). These protocols are surfaced to users during group travel emergencies.

## 1. Severe Nut Allergy (Anaphylaxis)
**Symptoms**: Swelling of lips/throat, severe difficulty breathing, hives, sudden drop in blood pressure.
**Protocol**:
1. Evaluate: Is the person having difficulty breathing?
2. If Yes -> Administer Epinephrine (EpiPen) immediately into the outer thigh.
3. Call Emergency Services (112/911) even if the EpiPen seems to have worked.
4. If no improvement after 5-15 minutes, administer a second EpiPen.

## 2. Diabetes (Hypoglycemia)
**Symptoms**: Shaking, sweating, confusion, irritability, unconsciousness.
**Protocol**:
1. Evaluate: Is the person conscious and able to swallow safely?
2. If Conscious -> Give 15g of fast-acting carbohydrates (e.g., glucose tablets, 4oz fruit juice, 1 tablespoon of sugar).
3. Wait 15 minutes. If symptoms persist, repeat 15g carbs.
4. If Unconscious -> **DO NOT** give anything by mouth. Administer Glucagon injection if available and trained. Call emergency services immediately.

## 3. Epilepsy (Seizures)
**Symptoms**: Convulsions, loss of consciousness, staring spells.
**Protocol**:
1. Protect the person from injury (clear the area of sharp objects, place something soft under their head).
2. **DO NOT** restrain them or put anything in their mouth.
3. Time the seizure.
4. Call emergency services if the seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes, if they have difficulty breathing afterwards, or if they request medical help.
5. Roll the person onto their side (recovery position) once convulsions stop.

## 4. Asthma Exacerbation
**Symptoms**: Severe wheezing, shortness of breath, chest tightness, panic.
**Protocol**:
1. Have the person sit upright (do not lay them down).
2. Assist them in taking their reliever inhaler (usually blue) using a spacer if available.
3. Dose: 1 puff every 30-60 seconds, up to a maximum of 10 puffs.
4. Call emergency services if symptoms do not improve after 10 puffs, or if they worsen at any point.
